Methodology

Verification, Evidence, Classification, Quantification, and Recording Standards

Purpose

This page describes how the ledger detects, verifies, classifies, quantifies, and records two categories of observable impact:

  • Harm — Measurable effects on human life-years (LY) and ecosystem capacity (ECY)
  • Interference — Measurable actions affecting the availability, integrity, or operation of the ledger itself

The methodology is designed for independent verification and audit. It does not assign intent, determine legal liability, or evaluate the correctness of underlying claims.

Core Principles

  • Verify, then inscribe: No entry is recorded without sufficient evidence.
  • Primary evidence first: Raw artifacts are preserved before interpretation.
  • Intent multiplies impact: Deliberate harm weights higher than negligence.
  • Immutability: Records are append-only, never deleted (retraction with explanation only).
  • Public verifiability: Every entry links to cryptographic evidence hashes.
  • Non-inference: Inclusion does not imply guilt, criminal liability, or moral condemnation.

Part 1: Harm Accountability (LY / ECY)

What Is Measured

  • Life-Years (LY): Years of human life lost due to preventable institutional action or inaction
  • Ecosystem-Capacity-Years (ECY): Years of ecosystem function or regenerative capacity lost

Life-Years Calculation

Life-Years Lost = Life Expectancy (year, region) − Age at Death

Counted only where a plausible, evidence-supported causal link exists between institutional action and preventable death.

Example: A 25-year-old killed in Gaza (life expectancy 73) = −48 LY

Ecosystem-Capacity-Years (ECY)

One ECY represents one year of functional ecosystem service loss. Calculation methods include:

  • Carbon: Metric tons CO₂e × years of atmospheric persistence
  • Land: Hectares lost × years for natural regeneration
  • Biodiversity: Habitat disruption × species recovery timeframe

Intent Multipliers (harm_type enum)

Each entry receives a harm_type from the fixed taxonomy below. The multiplier is applied to raw LY/ECY values before recording.

harm_typeMultiplierDefinition
NEGLIGENCE1×Failed to prevent foreseeable harm
RECKLESSNESS2×Knew risk, proceeded anyway
DELIBERATE5×Intended the harm
SYSTEMIC8×Harm embedded in policy or institutional structure
COVER_UP10×Actively concealed evidence of harm

Incident Types (causation categories)

Each entry receives exactly one incident_type describing the causal mechanism:

Violence & Physical Harm

  • DIRECT_VIOLENCE — Shooting, execution, assault, use of force
  • MILITARY_CAMPAIGN — Organized military action with documented civilian impact
  • SYSTEMIC_POLICY — Death or harm from institutional policy (e.g., prison conditions, border policies)

Governance & Security Failures

  • NEGLIGENCE — Preventable death from inaction or failure to protect
  • SECURITY_FAILURE — Preventable intelligence or operational failure
  • GOVERNANCE_FAILURE — Systemic breakdown of governance functions
  • DEMOCRATIC_EROSION — Undermining democratic institutions, judicial independence, or civil liberties

Accountability & Rights Violations

  • LEGAL_SUPPRESSION — Lawsuits or legal actions to suppress accountability or evidence
  • INTIMIDATION — Threats against witnesses, victims, validators, or stewards
  • POLITICAL_SUPPRESSION — Targeting of political opponents, critics, or journalists
  • CORRUPTION_ALLEGATION — Documented corruption charges (bribery, fraud, breach of trust)

Environmental & Medical

  • ENVIRONMENTAL — Pollution, ecosystem destruction, carbon emissions, biodiversity loss
  • MEDICAL_DENIAL — Denied medical care, treatment, or insurance coverage

International Law

  • WAR_CRIMES_ALLEGATION — Documented violations of international humanitarian law
  • ILLEGAL_SETTLEMENTS — Territorial violations under international law

Surplus (Repair Actions — positive values)

  • DIPLOMATIC_SUCCESS — Agreements that reduce future harm or conflict
  • ECONOMIC_DEVELOPMENT — Measurable quality-of-life or poverty-reduction improvements
  • NATIONAL_SECURITY — Defense capabilities that protect human life
  • POLITICAL_STABILITY — Governance continuity with verified positive outcomes

Part 2: Interference Accountability (Attacks on the Ledger)

Interference records document actions that materially affect ledger availability, integrity, input quality, legitimacy, or operational continuity.

Detection

Events may be detected through:

  • Automated monitoring (traffic anomalies, uptime loss, latency spikes)
  • Infrastructure notifications (hosting, registrar, payment processor, DNS provider)
  • Documented public actions (court filings, official statements, takedown notices)

Interference Classification (What occurred)

  1. Suppression — Blocking access (DDoS, ISP block, DNS filtering, censorship)
  2. Sabotage — Data corruption, deletion attempts, or unauthorized modification
  3. Disinformation — Coordinated narratives to delegitimize or discredit the ledger
  4. Flooding — Overwhelming with noise, spam, or bogus submissions
  5. Infiltration — Unauthorized access attempts against guardians, validators, or infrastructure
  6. Delegitimization — Legal or propaganda attacks on ledger authority or legitimacy
  7. Intimidation — Direct threats, doxxing, or harassment of stewards, witnesses, or validators
  8. Obstruction — Infrastructure or economic interference (payment processor block, registrar hold, hosting takedown)

Impact is quantified based on scale, duration, reversibility, and coordination level.

Multi-Vector Escalation

If the same entity employs two or more interference categories within 30 days:

Total = Base Debt × (1 + (n − 1) × 0.5)

Example: 3 categories → 2.0× multiplier

Public Failure Premium

If interference becomes widely reported and results in increased attention to the ledger (Streisand effect): +50%

Part 3: Evidence Standards

Evidence Capture Requirements

  • UTC timestamps (detection and inscription)
  • Primary artifacts (raw logs, official filings, court notices, death certificates, UN reports)
  • Corroborating artifacts (screenshots, secondary logs, independent media, NGO reports)
  • Cryptographic hashes (SHA-256) of all artifacts
  • Distributed storage identifiers (IPFS / Arweave CIDs) where available

Publication Threshold

A public entry requires:

  • At least one primary artifact (log export, official notice, court filing, primary source document)
  • At least one corroborating artifact (screenshot, secondary log, independent report, media coverage)

If this threshold is not met, the event is logged internally but not publicly inscribed.

Confidence Levels

ConfidenceStandard
LOWAnecdotal, uncorroborated, or single source

Part 4: Validation & Publication

Validation Process

Each entry requires independent review by at least two validators who confirm:

  • Evidence sufficiency (meeting the primary + corroborating threshold)
  • Correct classification (harm_type, incident_type, or interference category)
  • Accurate quantification (raw LY/ECY values and multiplier application)
  • Neutral, non-inflammatory language

Validators may decline publication if standards are not met. Validators cannot be the same as the submitter.

Published Entry Contains

  • Date logged (UTC ISO format)
  • Category (Harm or Interference)
  • Neutral description
  • Evidence hashes and/or links
  • Quantified impact (raw × multiplier)
  • Running balance for the entity (where attributable)
  • Attribution confidence level (LOW/MEDIUM/HIGH)

Entity States

StateMeaning

Part 5: Balance & Repair Mechanics

Outstanding Debt Calculation

For each entity, outstanding debt is calculated as:

Outstanding_LY = Σ(Harm_LY × Intent_Multiplier) + Σ(Surplus_LY)

Outstanding_ECY = Σ(Harm_ECY × Intent_Multiplier) + Σ(Surplus_ECY)

  • Harm values are negative (loss)
  • Surplus values are positive (repair)
  • A negative outstanding balance = debt remains
  • A zero or positive outstanding balance = REPAIRED status

Worked Example (Netanyahu — simplified)

  • Gaza military campaign: −8,500,000 LY × 8× (SYSTEMIC) = −68,000,000 LY
  • Abraham Accords (surplus): +5,800,000 LY
  • Outstanding = −68,000,000 + 5,800,000 = −62,200,000 LY (ACCRUING)

Debt Reduction (Surplus)

Debt may only be reduced through verifiable public repair actions:

  • Policy reforms that measurably save future lives
  • Diplomatic agreements that reduce conflict or harm
  • Environmental restoration or carbon removal
  • Restitution, reparations, or accountability mechanisms
  • Institutional reforms that prevent recurrence

Surplus does not auto-accrue. It must be claimed and verified through the same evidence standards as harm.

Status Determination

ConditionStatus
Outstanding_LY ≥ 0 AND Outstanding_ECY ≥ 0REPAIRED
Any surplus exists but outstanding < 0STABILIZED
No surplus, outstanding < 0ACCRUING

Years to Repair (Estimate)

If outstanding debt is negative and surplus activity exists:

Years to Repair = |Outstanding_LY| / (Average annual surplus over last 5 years)

Estimates are informational only and do not affect the ledger balance.

Part 6: Disputes & Retraction

Challenge Process

Any recorded entry may be disputed with new evidence. Dispute triggers re-validation by two new validators who were not involved in the original validation.

Retraction (Not Deletion)

If an entry is overturned:

  • The entry is marked "RETRACTED" with a public reason
  • The original entry remains visible (append-only principle)
  • Retracted entries do not contribute to outstanding debt
  • Historical balance recalculations are logged as corrections

Non-Inference Clause

Recording an interference act or harm does not imply:

  • Criminal guilt or legal liability
  • Moral condemnation or wrongdoing
  • Validity of any underlying political or legal claim

The ledger solely documents observable impact on humans, ecosystems, or this accounting process.

Absence Clause

The absence of records during a period implies only that no qualifying event meeting evidence thresholds was detected and verified. Absence does not imply absence of impact.

System Resilience

Verified harm or interference does not weaken the ledger.

Each recorded attempt to silence, obstruct, evade, or cause harm is absorbed, classified, quantified, and inscribed as permanent testimony.

The ledger becomes stronger with each recorded act.

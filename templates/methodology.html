Methodology

Verification, Evidence, Classification, Quantification, and Recording Standards

Purpose

This page describes how the ledger detects, verifies, classifies, quantifies, and records two categories of observable impact:

  • Harm — Measurable effects on human life-years (LY) and ecosystem capacity (ECY)
  • Interference — Measurable actions affecting the availability, integrity, or operation of the ledger

The methodology is designed for independent verification and audit. It does not assign intent, determine legal liability, or evaluate the correctness of underlying claims.

Core Principles

  • Verify, then inscribe: No entry is recorded without sufficient evidence.
  • Primary evidence first: Raw artifacts are preserved before interpretation.
  • Impact over narrative: Quantification is based on observable outcomes.
  • Immutability: Records are append-only and content-addressed.
  • Public verifiability: Evidence is independently checkable.
  • Non-inference: Inclusion does not imply guilt or wrongdoing.

Harm Accountability (LY / ECY)

What Is Measured

  • Life-Years (LY): Estimated years of human life lost due to preventable outcomes
  • Ecosystem-Capacity-Years (ECY): Estimated years of lost ecosystem function

Life-Years Calculation

Life-Years Lost = Regional Life Expectancy − Age at Death

Included only where a plausible, evidence-supported causal link exists.

Ecosystem-Capacity Definition

An Ecosystem-Capacity-Year (ECY) represents one year of functional ecosystem service (e.g., carbon sequestration, biodiversity support, regeneration).

  • Carbon: CO₂e × atmospheric persistence
  • Land: area × regeneration time
  • Biodiversity: disruption × recovery timeframe

Impact Multipliers

  • Negligence: 1×
  • Recklessness: 2×
  • Deliberate: 5×
  • Systemic: 8×
  • Concealment: 10×

Multipliers adjust magnitude but do not determine classification.

Interference Accountability

Interference records document actions that materially affect ledger availability, integrity, input quality, or operational continuity.

Detection

Events may be detected through automated monitoring (traffic anomalies, uptime loss), infrastructure notifications, or documented public actions such as court filings.

Classification

  1. Suppression — Blocking access (e.g., DDoS, censorship)
  2. Sabotage — Data corruption or deletion attempts
  3. Disinformation — Coordinated credibility attacks
  4. Flooding — Noise or spam injection
  5. Infiltration — Unauthorized access attempts
  6. Delegitimization — Legal or narrative challenges
  7. Intimidation — Threats to participants
  8. Obstruction — Infrastructure or financial disruption

Quantification

Interference impact is quantified relative to its effect on ledger operation and availability.

  • Suppression: 0.1–0.2×
  • Sabotage (failed): 0.05× | (reversed success): 1.0×
  • Disinformation: 0.3×
  • Flooding: 0.2×
  • Infiltration: 1.0× per target
  • Delegitimization: 0.3–1.0×
  • Intimidation: 0.2–1.0×
  • Obstruction: 0.2–2.0×

Multi-vector escalation: Additional categories increase total impact.
Amplification: Widely reported interference may increase measured impact.

Evidence Standards

  • UTC timestamps
  • Primary artifacts (logs, filings, notices)
  • Corroborating artifacts (independent reports, secondary logs)
  • Cryptographic hashes
  • Distributed storage identifiers (IPFS / Arweave)

Publication Threshold

Public entries require at least one primary artifact and one independent corroborating source.

Validation

Each entry is independently reviewed by at least two validators to confirm:

  • Evidence sufficiency
  • Correct classification
  • Accurate quantification
  • Neutral language

Balance & Repair

Total impact is tracked as a cumulative balance:

Balance = Σ(Harm × Multipliers) − Σ(Verified Repair Actions)

Repair actions (“Surplus”) include verifiable efforts that measurably reduce harm or restore capacity.

Non-Inference Clause

Recording an event does not imply legal liability, guilt, or moral judgment.

Absence Clause

Absence of records does not imply absence of impact.

System Behavior

The ledger records and preserves observable impact. Entries are append-only and remain subject to audit, challenge, and revision.

All recorded impact persists.
All balances are subject to repair.
The ledger remains open.

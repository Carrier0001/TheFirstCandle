Understanding LY and ECY

LY (Life Years) represent the impact of harm or surplus on human lives over time. They measure how events affect the length and quality of life across generations.

ECY (Ecological Years) represent the impact on ecosystems and environments. They measure how events affect ecological stability, sustainability, and long-term environmental health.

Harm Classifications

Each entry in the ledger is classified by intent. These categories help distinguish the nature of harm:

  • Negligence — harm caused by carelessness or failure to act responsibly.
  • Recklessness — harm caused by knowingly taking dangerous risks.
  • Deliberate — harm caused intentionally, with direct intent to damage or exploit.
  • Systemic — harm embedded in structures, policies, or institutions that perpetuate damage over time.
  • Cover‑up — harm compounded by concealment, denial, or obstruction of accountability.

These classifications are used internally to calculate multipliers, but for public understanding they simply describe the type of intent behind each entry.
